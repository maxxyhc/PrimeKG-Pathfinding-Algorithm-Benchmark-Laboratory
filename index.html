PrimeKG Pathfinding Algorithm Benchmark Laboratory

Benchmarking Graph Algorithms for Drug Repurposing Discovery

Project Overview

This project investigates and benchmarks pathfinding algorithms on the PrimeKG (Precision Medicine Knowledge Graph) to evaluate their effectiveness in discovering mechanistically plausible drug repurposing candidates.

We aim to answer a critical question: Can standard graph algorithms find biologically meaningful pathways, or do they fall into "shortcut traps" that produce statistically short but mechanistically meaningless paths?

Motivation

The Hub Trap

High-degree nodes like TP53 (2,670 connections) or UBC (10,396 connections) act as "shortcuts" that algorithms prefer, but these paths lack biological specificity.

The Drug Trap

Naive algorithms often route through other drugs as intermediates, producing paths like "Drug A ‚Üí Drug B ‚Üí Disease" that are pharmacologically meaningless.

The Disease Trap

Paths may jump through unrelated diseases that share genetic risk factors, creating spurious associations without mechanistic basis.

Mechanistic Explanation

  1. Sildenafil inhibits PDE5A (Phosphodiesterase 5A), preventing the breakdown of cGMP.
  2. PDE5A is part of the cGMP effects pathway, which regulates smooth muscle relaxation.
  3. The cGMP effects pathway is downstream of Nitric oxide stimulates guanylate cyclase, which produces cGMP.
  4. This pathway involves NOS3 (Endothelial Nitric Oxide Synthase), which produces nitric oxide in blood vessels.
  5. NOS3 dysfunction is associated with Erectile Dysfunction due to impaired vasodilation.

Methodology

  1. Curate Ground Truth Pathways: Collect validated drug-mechanism-disease pathways from literature and pharmacological databases.
  2. Implement Pathfinding Algorithms: Test BFS, Dijkstra, A*, bidirectional search, and constrained variants on PrimeKG.
  3. Benchmark Against Ground Truth: Measure how often algorithms recover the mechanistically correct pathway vs. taking shortcuts.
  4. Develop Quality Metrics: Score paths based on biological plausibility, hub avoidance, and mechanistic coherence.
